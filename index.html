b) Efficiency Points are an additional indicator reflecting the positive or negative win rate of each hero. If a hero has a positive win rate under specific conditions, he gets +1 point. These points help you understand how successful each of your heroes is in the current draft.

Don't forget about counterpicks and synergy. These indicators show how well your heroes interact with each other and how much they can neutralize threats from enemy heroes. If you have strong synergy and counterpicks, it adds extra confidence to your choice.

Game Tempo Theory or Power Spike

Game Tempo Theory

Game Tempo Theory is a strategy that determines how a team manages its potential throughout the game. It is akin to the "lifeblood" of a team, its primary driving force. This theory suggests that at certain moments during a match, a team can reach its maximum potential, or "power spike," and then gradually lose effectiveness.

The tempo of the game and team efficiency are closely linked. Some teams strive to maintain a high tempo throughout the entire match, constantly pressuring their opponents and not giving them time to recover. Others prefer a more cautious approach, conserving their strength for critical moments when their power spike will be most significant.

What is a Power Spike?

A power spike can be explained very simply: it is the moment in the game when a team has the greatest chance to turn the situation in its favor. It is the moment when your heroes have maximum impact and can deliver a decisive blow to the enemy.

Let's consider some examples:

1. Wraith King (WK) vs Bloodseeker: By the 20-minute mark, Bloodseeker has already lost its late-game potential. Meanwhile, Wraith King can purchase Armlet and Desolator by this time, becoming fully ready for fights. Thus, WK reaches its power spike exactly at this moment, while Bloodseeker starts to weaken.

2. Dark Seer vs Broodmother: The timings here are roughly the same. Broodmother loses its late-game potential by the 20-minute mark, while Dark Seer only reaches its tempo. Additionally, Dark Seer serves as a direct counter to Broodmother due to the ability to throw shields on spiders. However, it's important to understand that the tempo itself plays a crucial role. Dark Seer becomes stronger as the game progresses, whereas Broodmother loses its effectiveness.

3. Timbersaw vs Doom: The situation here is slightly different. Doom leans more towards the 30-minute mark but remains stronger than Timbersaw in the late game thanks to its carry potential. Nevertheless, Timbersaw also has its own advantages in the early stages, such as great mobility and the ability to quickly move around the map. This makes it a dangerous opponent in the early game, but its effectiveness decreases as the match progresses.

Why Understanding Power Spikes is Important

Although the theory of power spikes does not work in every single case (Dota 2 is too chaotic), it remains an essential tool for analysis, especially when teams are evenly matched. In 70% of cases, if teams are on an equal level, key moments of the game occur precisely when one side reaches its power spike.

Interesting Fact

Practically no one in Dota 2 fully understands the power spike of their team. However, for those who place bets on matches, this knowledge is extremely important. If your favorite team can overcome human factors and wait for the moment when its power spike becomes decisive, it significantly increases the chances of victory.

The theory of game tempo and power spikes helps you better understand when and how a team can use its strengths.
